The account of Lehi and his wife Sariah and his four sons
1I, Nephi, having been born of good parents, therefore I was taught somewhat in all the learning of my father;
and having seen many afflictions in the course of my days,
nevertheless, having been highly favored of the Lord in all my days —
yes, having had a great knowledge of the goodness and the mysteries of God,
therefore I make a record of my proceedings in my days.
2Yes, I make a record in the language of my father,
which consists of the learning of the Jews and the language of the Egyptians.
3And I know that the record which I make is true;
and I make it with my own hand;
and I make it according to my knowledge.
4For it came to pass in the beginning of the first year of the reign of Zedekiah, king of Judah
(my father, Lehi, having lived at Jerusalem in all his days);
and in that same year there came many prophets,
prophesying to the people that they must repent, or the great city Jerusalem must be destroyed.
5That is why my father, Lehi, as he went out, prayed to the Lord,
yes, even with all his heart, on behalf of his people.
6And it came to pass that as he prayed to the Lord,
a pillar of fire came and sat upon a rock before him;
and he saw and heard much;
and because of the things which he saw and heard
he did shake and tremble exceedingly.
7And it came to pass that he returned to his own house at Jerusalem;
and he threw himself upon his bed, being overcome with the Spirit and the things which he had seen.
8And being thus overcome with the Spirit,
he was carried away in a vision,
even that he saw the heavens open,
and he thought he saw God sitting upon his throne,
surrounded by numberless groups of angels in the attitude of singing and praising their God.
