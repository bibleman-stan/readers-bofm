About This Edition

This is a reading edition of the Book of Mormon. The original text is preserved exactly, but it has been reformatted into shorter sense-lines that follow natural pauses in speech. This makes the text easier to read aloud, easier to follow visually, and more accessible for people who are learning English or encountering the Book of Mormon for the first time.

Tap the book and chapter name at the top of the screen to open the book picker, where you can jump to any chapter. Tap the menu icon at the top right to open reading settings. Everything is designed to stay out of your way until you need it.

Reading Settings

Modern words
Old-fashioned or archaic words are swapped for modern equivalents (for example, "wherefore" becomes "therefore," "unto" becomes "to"). The original word is always preserved underneath — tap any green-underlined word to see it.
Listen
Shows an audio player at the bottom of the screen for the current chapter. Press play to hear the chapter narrated aloud while the text highlights line by line. The player has play/pause, skip, and speed controls (0.75x to 2x). Tap any line while narration is active to jump to that point in the audio. When a chapter ends, the next one starts automatically.
Section headings
Adds section headings throughout the text. These short, descriptive titles mark natural breaks in the narrative or teaching — like chapter titles in a novel. They help you see the structure of each book and understand where one thought ends and the next begins.
Text size
Choose Small, Medium, or Large to adjust the reading text size to your preference.

Other Features

Search
The magnifying glass icon opens full-text search across the entire Book of Mormon. Searching "pray" also finds "praying" and "prayed." Use quotes for exact phrases, or Boolean operators (AND, OR, NOT). Results are grouped by book, and tapping a result jumps to that verse.
Book introductions
When you open the book picker and tap a book name, a brief introduction appears below the chapter numbers explaining who the narrator is and what to expect.
Starred notes
A small colored star before a word or phrase means a brief note is available — tap the phrase to read it. Notes cover place names, cultural practices, historical references, and word meanings that modern readers may not recognize.

The Book of Mormon Reading Edition

A new way into the Book of Mormon

If you find the Book of Mormon hard to read, difficult to understand, or unfulfilling, this edition seeks to give you new tools that can help. Old words are replaced with modern ones, and the text is broken into shorter lines that reflect the ideas in the text. Additionally, the text has a fresh audio narration that can help it come alive.
