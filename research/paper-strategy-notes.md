## Venue Strategy

### Primary target: JBMS (Journal of Book of Mormon Studies)
- Peer-reviewed, academically rigorous, the prestige tier for BofM studies
- Where the Roper/Fields/Schaalje stylometric work appeared
- Puts you directly in conversation with Hilton's wordprint studies
- Right intellectual level and peer review structure

### Secondary options:
- **BYU Studies** — broader LDS audience, high visibility, less peer-reviewed pressure. Better as a *second* publication — accessible version after the methodology is established
- **Digital Humanities Quarterly / DSH** — completely separate map, establishes you in DH professionally. Requires full quantitative apparatus
- **Interpreter** — high volume, wide readership, but somewhat discounted by academics as advocacy-adjacent

### Strategic recommendation:
Draft for JBMS first (humanities register). Lead with the secular DH venue if maximizing cross-field visibility is the priority. A paper that has already appeared in a secular peer-reviewed venue carries credibility into the JBMS conversation that doesn't flow in reverse.

## Paper Shape Decision

### Paper A — The FEF Paper (recommended first)
One focused discovery, rigorously demonstrated. Publishable sooner, cleaner argument. Gets cited more precisely ("the paper that identified FEFs").

### Paper B — The Method Paper
The story of building colometric stylometry as an analytical tool. More ambitious, higher bar. Requires the full quantitative analysis to be complete.

### Recommended approach:
Write Paper A first. Near the conclusion, include: "The broader analytical framework — consistent editorial colometry applied at scale as a tool for stylometric investigation — produces additional findings that will be reported separately." This plants the flag for Paper B without overextending Paper A.

The method and the finding are mutually reinforcing: FEFs are credible because the method that found them is consistent; the method is credible because it found something as clean as FEFs.

## Writing Register for JBMS

Write in humanities register, not IMRD:
- Headers describe content, not function ("Front-End Frames in Mormon's Abridgment" not "Results")
- Methodology woven into the argument, not isolated
- Quantitative findings presented as evidence within prose, not standalone results block
- Must enter the existing conversation explicitly and early

### Descriptive section headers (example):
1. Introduction
2. "And It Came to Pass": The Problem of the Phrase
3. Previous Approaches
4. The Editorial Method: Atomic Images and Breath Units
5. Front-End Frames: Definition and Examples
6. The Distribution of FEFs Across Attributed Sections
7. The Hebrew Parallel and Its Significance
8. Implications and Conclusion

## Informal Conventions (Welch/Alter/Hardy Model)

1. **The phenomenon leads, the theory follows.** Show a specific FEF before defining FEF formally.
2. **The close reading IS the argument.** Examples carry the weight; statistics confirm.
3. **Jargon coined sparingly and in plain English.** Name arrives after reader already recognizes the pattern.
4. **Hebrew parallel handled with restraint.** Contextualizing and corroborating, not foundational.
5. **Implications discussed briefly with deliberate hedging.** "Suggests," "may indicate," "one interpretation consistent with..."
6. **Footnotes do real work.** Main text readable by non-specialists; scholarly apparatus in notes.
7. **Tone is curious, not defensive.** No pre-emptive apology for subject matter.

### Structure skeleton (from all three scholars):
1. Open with a specific textual puzzle or observation
2. Survey previous scholarship, briefly
3. Introduce analytical approach by demonstrating it on one clear example
4. Accumulate examples until the pattern is undeniable
5. Name and define the pattern formally
6. Show the pattern holds at scale
7. Note the ancient/broader literary parallel, briefly
8. State implications carefully and with restraint
9. Identify further work the finding calls for

## Handling the Secularist Objection

The sharpest null hypothesis: "You've shown Joseph Smith was a more sophisticated author than Twain gave him credit for."

### Responses (in order of strength):
1. **Concede directly and reframe.** The finding is interesting under either hypothesis. Single-author = extraordinary deliberate craft worth studying.
2. **The consistency problem.** Deliberate stylistic variation by a single author typically shows the author's own era bleeding through. FEF density is not an obvious variable a writer consciously varies.
3. **The convergence argument** (future work). If colometric and lexical stylometry converge on the same clustering, that compound coincidence demands explanation.
4. **The Isaiah control.** Under single authorship, Isaiah sections were rendered knowing they were Isaiah — not fictional voices. If Isaiah shows a distinct colometric profile paralleling Hebrew conventions, that's evidence the structural signal tracks source material.

### Framing that handles it:
"The structural differentiation documented here admits of at least two interpretations. Under a multiple-authorship hypothesis, FEF profiles may preserve compositional signatures of distinct source voices. Under a single-authorship hypothesis, they constitute evidence of sustained and measurable stylistic differentiation across fictional voices — a literary achievement whose consistency and depth warrants further investigation in either case."

## Quantitative Analysis Requirements

### Level 1 — Must Have:
- Raw FEF count per attributed section
- FEF density (per 100 verses or per 1000 sense-lines)
- Comparison across sections: Nephi narrative, Mormon narrative, sermonic speech, divine speech, Isaiah, chronicle

### Level 2 — Should Have:
- Mean/median FEF line length (words) per section
- FEF discourse marker distribution per section
- Expansion line count per FEF

### Level 3 — Good to Have (needed for DH venue):
- Line length histogram per section
- Bimodality coefficient (values above 0.555 suggest bimodality)

### The make-or-break number:
FEF density in Mormon's narrative abridgment vs. FEF density in Nephi's narrative chapters. If the ratio is 2.5:1 or higher, the paper has a clear quantitative anchor.

## Connection to OTC Dissertation

The paper establishes empirically that:
- Consistent colometric analysis at scale produces structurally significant findings
- Those findings correlate with attributed authorial voice
- The method is documented, replicable, and falsifiable

This is not a footnote in the dissertation — it's the empirical foundation the theoretical argument stands on. When you argue that FEFs are "mnemonic load-bearing walls," you're extending a finding already established in peer-reviewed literature.

**Intellectual arc:** Paper establishes the phenomenon. Dissertation explains it.
